About Me

Hi! My name is John Chong and I'm a prospective software engineer! Born and raised in San Jose, CA, I'm very familiar with beautiful weather and overpriced gas. I was in the Bay Area for the first 21 years of my life; I also spent my undergrad years at UC Berkeley where I studied Data Science. Shortly after graduating, I found myself relocating to St.Louis, Missouri for work where I wanted to spend some time outside of the bubble I grew up around and I have been thoroughly enjoying the past 2 years in the Midwest. Before realizing I wanted to pursue this software engineer career I worked as an actuary: actuaries specialize in risk management and statistics to reasonably project future losses for companies. In other words, I worked in a consulting firm whose clients are insurance companies. After working in that industry for around 1.5 years, I realized that I wasn't really being challenged in a technical standpoint and that the projected path of the actuarial career wasn't a fit for me. So while it was a big decision, it wasn't a hard decision to try something new and explore a new field while I still had the ability and time to.

But enough of my career, to prove to you that I'm a regular human being I do have interests that I would love to share with you! I'm a HUGE basketball fan; I was a little late when I started watching in 2017, but I'm proudly a part of Laker Nation. It might be weird given that I'm from the Bay Area, but that's something you can ask about later. While I may not be the best, I really enjoy playing basketball with friends or in pickup. Being the active guy I am, I also played volleyball in high school and enjoy working out and running. I do consider LeBron James the GOAT of basketball, so we can open that can of worms another time as well. In contrast with my outdoor inclinations, I'm also a huge coffee fanatic; I enjoy brewing my own coffee, visitng cafes, and exploring new brewing methods and beans. You can take a look at the playlist I've compiled that I love to brew with (Apple Music guy here, #sorrynotsorry). Besides coding, I've recently be into a lot of reading; typically theological books, productivity books, or inspirational autobiographies; I'm appreciating the concept of winding down. Maybe it comes with age? Regardless, there's only so much a personal website can do with introductions, but I'm really glad you got all the way down here. I'm looking forward to connecting with you in the near future and feel free to check out my resume!
